# Block Business Banking Stakeholder Meeting Templates

## Finance Leadership Briefing Template

### Meeting Objectives
- Understand overall business banking strategy and performance
- Identify key challenges and opportunities
- Clarify budget and resource allocation
- Establish reporting cadence and success metrics

### Key Questions to Ask

#### Strategic Context
1. What are Block's 3-year goals for the business banking segment?
2. How does business banking fit into Block's overall ecosystem strategy?
3. What's our current market position vs. competitors?
4. What are the biggest opportunities for growth?

#### Financial Performance
1. What are the current P&L metrics for each product line?
2. How do we measure and report profitability by product?
3. What are our customer acquisition cost targets and actuals?
4. How do we allocate shared costs across products?

#### Resource Allocation
1. What's the current budget allocation for business banking?
2. Where are we investing for growth vs. maintenance?
3. What are the key resource constraints?
4. How do we prioritize product enhancements?

### Data Requests
- [ ] Historical P&L statements (24 months)
- [ ] Current budget and forecast models
- [ ] Customer acquisition metrics
- [ ] Competitive analysis reports
- [ ] Strategic planning documents

## Product Team Deep Dive Template

### Instant Transfer Product Meeting

#### Product Specifications
1. What's our current fee structure and how does it compare to competitors?
2. What are the average transaction values and frequencies?
3. What processing partners do we use and what are the costs?
4. What's our current market share and growth rate?

#### Operational Metrics
1. What are our processing success rates and error rates?
2. What are the key cost drivers (technology, compliance, support)?
3. How do we handle fraud prevention and what are the costs?
4. What's our customer satisfaction and Net Promoter Score?

#### Future Roadmap
1. What product enhancements are planned for the next 12 months?
2. What new market segments are we targeting?
3. What regulatory changes are on the horizon?
4. What technology investments are needed?

### Credit Card Product Meeting

#### Revenue Model
1. What's our current interchange rate structure?
2. How do annual fees and interest rates compare to market?
3. What's our current charge-off rate by customer segment?
4. How do we price risk and set credit limits?

#### Risk Management
1. What are our underwriting criteria and approval rates?
2. How do we monitor and manage credit risk?
3. What's our fraud detection and prevention strategy?
4. How do regulatory capital requirements affect profitability?

### Debit Card Product Meeting

#### Transaction Economics
1. What's our current interchange rate and how is it trending?
2. What are the key transaction volume drivers?
3. How do ATM partnerships work and what are the economics?
4. What are our processing costs per transaction?

#### Customer Behavior
1. What's the average monthly spend per customer?
2. How do customers use the product (online vs. in-store)?
3. What features drive customer engagement?
4. How does debit card usage correlate with other Block products?

## Operations & Risk Meeting Template

### Risk Management Discussion
1. What are our current fraud rates and trends by product?
2. How do we measure and manage operational risk?
3. What are our key compliance requirements and costs?
4. What risk management tools and systems do we use?

### Operational Efficiency
1. What are our key operational metrics (processing time, error rates)?
2. Where are our operational bottlenecks or constraints?
3. How scalable is our current infrastructure?
4. What are our customer service costs and satisfaction metrics?

### Regulatory Environment
1. What regulatory changes are coming that will affect us?
2. How do we manage compliance costs across products?
3. What are our relationships with regulators and auditors?
4. How do we stay competitive while maintaining compliance?

## Data & Analytics Meeting Template

## Meeting Preparation Checklist

### Before Each Meeting
- [ ] Review attendee backgrounds and roles
- [ ] Prepare specific questions based on their expertise
- [ ] Bring copies of preliminary analysis or frameworks
- [ ] Set clear expectations for data sharing and follow-up

### During Each Meeting
- [ ] Take detailed notes on key metrics and insights
- [ ] Ask for specific data sources and contacts
- [ ] Clarify any assumptions or methodologies
- [ ] Schedule follow-up meetings if needed

### After Each Meeting
- [ ] Send summary notes to attendees
- [ ] Follow up on data requests within 24 hours
- [ ] Update financial models with new information
- [ ] Schedule any additional meetings needed
